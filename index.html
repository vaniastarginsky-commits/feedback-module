OUROBOROS

A stereo feedback instrument for Eurorack built around reroutable clipping, SSI2144 filtering, external or internal loop processing, loop-end tone control, and performable switch logic.

Explore the panel See the signal flow

OUROBOROS is built as a compact stereo feedback laboratory: move the diode clipper or SSI2144 filter between the main path and the loop, patch external processors through send/return, or let the internal PT2399 delay keep the recursion alive when nothing is inserted.

It works both as a sound-design tool and as a performance instrument: loop-end HP/LP/Phase cleanup keeps feedback musical, CV reaches key controls, switch logic reshapes topology in real time, and the reversible symbol/text panel supports two reading styles.

Performance logic

How SW logic works

This section reads like a compact operating guide: short press, long press, trigger assignment, and delay behavior. It describes the current product concept rather than an outdated implementation.

SW1

Short press moves the clipper between the main path and the loop. Long press assigns the global trigger input to SW1.

  • Bi-colour indicator.
  • Acts on routing, not on level.

SW2

Short press moves the SSI2144 section between the main path and the loop. Long press assigns the global trigger input to SW2.

  • Bi-colour indicator.
  • Changes tone and behavior of the loop, not just color.

SW3

Short press changes the macro topology: LOOP versus SERIES. In LOOP mode, the phase block returns into the crossfader wet input. In SERIES mode, the feedback loop is broken and the return chain ends at the output.

  • Bi-colour indicator.
  • Long press assigns the global trigger input to SW3.

SW4

Short press sends a tap-tempo event. Long press assigns the global trigger input to SW4. Extra-long press toggles the internal delay on or off.

  • Mono LED with activity feedback.
  • Tap tempo can come from the button or from the assigned trigger input.
  • PT2399-based delay time is controlled by digital potentiometer under MCU control.
Patch ideas

Use cases

A few practical starting points for exploring the module as a feedback instrument, an insert hub, or a self-contained loop processor.

Stereo feedback color

Keep SW1 and SW2 in LOOP, leave SW3 in LOOP, and use Feedback Mix as the threshold between gentle widening and unstable recursion. LP and HP keep the loop playable instead of just runaway.

External loop processor

Use SEND/RETURN for reverb, resonator, pitch, or another distortion. The module still gives you post-loop HP/LP and Phase cleanup, plus the ability to move clipper or SSI2144 before or inside the loop.

Self-contained fallback delay

When no external S/R processor is used, the internal PT2399 delay can occupy that role. SW4 becomes a playable delay control: tap for tempo, hold for assignment, extra-long for on/off.
